We don't deserve Club World Cup bonus - Guardiola.

Manchester City boss Pep Guardiola does not believe his squad deserve any of the £97m prize money the club would pocket by winning the Club World Cup.

World governing body Fifa has announced the performance-related rewards for this summer's expanded 32-team event in the United States. Europe's major clubs are expected to get the lion's share, with both City and Chelsea - who qualified by winning the Champions League in 2023 and 2020 respectively - in line to collect the biggest prize pot in club football by winning it.

Barcelona close in on LaLiga title after win over Osasuna

Barcelona overcame LaLiga's controversial decision to schedule a late match on gameweek 27 just after the end of the World Cup qualifiers and Nations League quarter-finals.

Hansi Flick's side beat Osasuna 3-0 with goals from Ferrán Torres, Dani Olmo and Robert Lewandowski at the Montjuic Olympic Stadium. Even without Raphinha (rest due to physical exhaustion), Barcelona needed just 11 minutes to open the scoring, with Baldé finding Ferrán Torres on a cross after a beautiful exchange of passes.
